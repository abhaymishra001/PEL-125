2. Detailed Handwritten Notes
▼
🟦 A. Present Tenses
1️⃣ Present Simple
Used to express general truths, habits, facts, and permanent states. The action is regular or always true.
Structure: S + V1 (add -s/-es for He/She/It)
  • General truths → The sun rises in the east.
  • Daily habits → She drinks coffee every morning.
  • Permanent states → He works at a hotel.
  • Timetable future → The train leaves at 6 AM.
🧠 Trick: If the sentence talks about "always / usually / every day / never / sometimes" → use Present Simple!
❌ "She is knowing the answer." ✅ "She knows the answer." — Stative verbs don't use continuous!
2️⃣ Present Continuous
Used for actions happening right now, temporary situations, annoying habits (+always), and future arrangements.
Structure: S + am/is/are + V-ing
  • Happening now → She is reading a newspaper upstairs.
  • Temporary → She is working at the hotel until next week.
  • Annoying habit → She is always singing in the shower!
  • Future arrangement → She is getting married on 3 November.
🚫 Stative (Non-Action) Verbs — NEVER use in continuous form!
Possession:
own, belong, have, possess
Opinion:
know, believe, think, understand
Feelings:
like, love, want, need, prefer
Senses:
see, hear, smell*, taste*, feel*
⭐ Starred verbs* CAN be dynamic when used for voluntary actions: "I am tasting the soup" (voluntary action) vs "This tastes horrible" (perception).
Aspect Present Simple Present Continuous
Use General truths, habits, facts Actions happening NOW / temporary
Structure S + V1 (-s/-es) S + am/is/are + V-ing
Signal Words always, usually, every day, never now, at the moment, currently, still
Example It rarely rains in the desert. She is reading upstairs now.
Habits Regular habits: He jogs daily. Annoying habits (+always): He is always losing his keys!
Future use Scheduled events: The bus leaves at 8. Arrangements: I am meeting him tomorrow.
3️⃣ Present Perfect
Links past to present. Used when WHEN is not important, only the RESULT matters. Shows experience, achievement, or recent action.
Structure: S + have/has + V3 (past participle)
  • Experience → I have seen the Eiffel Tower. (when doesn't matter)
  • Unfinished period → I have eaten 10 pizzas this week.
  • Achievements → I have written two books.
  • With ever/never → Have you ever been to Japan?
Ever vs Never ✅ Have you ever played basketball?
✅ I have never been to Spain.
4️⃣ Present Perfect Continuous
Focuses on the DURATION / activity of an action that started in the past and continues (or just stopped). Whether completed doesn't matter.
Structure: S + have/has + been + V-ing
  • Duration focus → She has been painting her bedroom.
  • Ongoing → I have been playing football since 3 o'clock.
  • Evidence of activity → He has been repairing the car for 2 hours.
Aspect Present Perfect Present Perfect Continuous
Emphasis RESULT — something is completed ACTIVITY / DURATION — ongoing process
Structure have/has + V3 have/has + been + V-ing
Signal Words for, since, already, ever, never, yet, still all day, for 4 years, how long?, the whole week
Example She has painted her bedroom. ✅ She has been painting her bedroom. ✅
Focus He has repaired the car. (done!) He has been repairing the car for 2 hours.
🟥 B. Past Tenses
5️⃣ Past Simple
Used for completed actions at a SPECIFIC TIME in the past. Both action AND time are finished. Also used for 2nd type conditionals.
Structure: S + V2 (past form) / S + did + V1
  • Specific time → They went to the cinema yesterday.
  • Habit in past → When I was little, I always visited my grandparents.
  • Sequence of events → I saw the Eiffel Tower in 2007.
  • 2nd Conditional → If I were you, I'd go.
Key words: yesterday, ago, last week/year, in 2019, when, if
6️⃣ Past Continuous
Describes an action in progress at a specific time in the past. Often used with simple past to show interrupted action.
Structure: S + was/were + V-ing
  • In progress → I was sitting in the café watching people.
  • Interrupted → I was watching YouTube when the phone rang.
  • Parallel actions → They were watching TV while I was studying.
  • Specific past time → Mike was cooking dinner last night at 7 pm.
🧠 Trick: Past Continuous = background action (was doing). Past Simple = interrupting action (did).
Example: I was walking (cont.) when it rained (simple).
Aspect Past Simple Past Continuous
Focus COMPLETION of action DURATION of action
Structure S + V2 S + was/were + V-ing
Signal Words yesterday, ago, last, when, in 2020 while, as, at 9am, all morning, when (interruptor)
Example The thief stole the bag and ran. I was sitting at the café.
7️⃣ Past Perfect
Shows action that was COMPLETED BEFORE another past action. The "earlier past." Used to show sequence of past events.
Structure: S + had + V3 (past participle)
  • Earlier action → When he arrived, the train had left.
  • Result of past → I met them after they had divorced.
  • Linked events → I could not take my flight because I had forgotten my boarding pass.
🧠 Past Perfect = The event that happened FIRST when telling a past story.
Timeline: had done → then → did
8️⃣ Past Perfect Continuous
Describes an action that started in the past and continued until another past time. Emphasizes duration before a past event.
Structure: S + had + been + V-ing
  • Duration before event → I had been waiting for hours when my mother arrived.
  • Cause of past state → He needed a holiday because he had been working hard for six months.
  • I had been travelling all night, so I was tired.
Aspect Past Perfect Past Perfect Continuous
Emphasis RESULT of past action (completed) DURATION of past action
Structure had + V3 had + been + V-ing
Example I had been to London twice. Sara had been working here for two weeks when she had the accident.
🟩 C. Future Tenses
9️⃣ Simple Future (Will)
Used for spontaneous decisions, predictions, promises, and future facts. Decided at the moment of speaking.
Structure: S + will + V1
  • Spontaneous decision → I forgot my lunch. I will grab something.
  • Prediction (opinion) → I think United will win the game.
  • Promise → I will help you with your project.
  • Future fact → The sun will rise tomorrow.
🔟 Future with "Going To"
Used for prior plans/intentions (decided before speaking) and predictions based on present evidence.
Structure: S + am/is/are + going to + V1
  • Prior plan → I'm going to visit my friend tonight.
  • Evidence-based prediction → Look at those black clouds. It is going to rain.
  • Intention → I'm going to start my diet on Monday.
⭐ Will vs Going To: "It's going to rain" (you can see clouds = evidence). "I think it will rain" (just your opinion/prediction without evidence).
Aspect WILL BE GOING TO
Decision time AT the moment of speaking (spontaneous) BEFORE the moment of speaking (pre-planned)
Prediction Based on personal opinion: "I think it will snow." Based on present evidence: "It's going to snow." (dark clouds!)
Promises/Offers Yes: "I will help you." Not typically
Example I'll have salad now. (just decided) I'm going to visit my aunt next Friday. (planned earlier)
1️⃣1️⃣ Future Continuous
Action that will be in progress at a specific future time.
Structure: S + will be + V-ing
  • I will be studying tomorrow at this time.
  • She will be working late tonight.
  • They will be playing soccer.
1️⃣2️⃣ Future Perfect & Future Perfect Continuous
Future Perfect: Action completed BY a certain future point.
Future Perfect Continuous: Action will continue up to a certain future point (focus on duration).
S + will have + V3   |   S + will have been + V-ing
  • Fut. Perfect → I will have finished my homework by 5 PM.
  • Fut. Perf. Cont. → James will have been teaching at the university for more than a year by the time he leaves.
🧠 Future Perfect = completed BEFORE a future deadline.
Future Perf. Continuous = ongoing action whose DURATION is emphasized up to a future point.
🔄 Present Continuous with FUTURE Meaning
When talking about arrangements already made, use Present Continuous (NOT Present Simple):
What are you doing tomorrow evening?
I'm going to the theatre.
Ann is coming tomorrow.

🌟 KEY EXAM POINTS to Remember
  • Stative verbs (know, love, want, own) → NEVER use -ing form
  • Present Perfect = past action with present result; WHEN not important
  • Past Simple = WHEN is important (specific time)
  • Will = spontaneous decision; Going To = pre-planned
  • Past Perfect = action done BEFORE another past action
  • Continuous tenses = focus on DURATION; Simple tenses = focus on COMPLETION
  • Perfect Continuous = focus on ACTIVITY ongoing or just stopped
  • "Ever" → used in questions; "Never" → negative statement (both with Present Perfect)
