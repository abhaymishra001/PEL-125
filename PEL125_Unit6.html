Part A: IDIOMS
📌 Definition: An idiom is a fixed phrase or expression whose meaning cannot be understood from the literal meanings of the individual words. It is a figurative expression used naturally by native speakers.
Example: "Hit the books" ≠ physically hitting books → means "to study"
💡 Memory Trick (3F Rule): Idioms are Fixed, Figurative & Frequently used. You can't change the words and understand only the overall meaning, not individual words!
🔵 Why Use Idioms?
  • Make speech colorful and interesting
  • Sound like a natural, fluent English speaker
  • Express complex ideas in short phrases
  • Commonly tested in exams, interviews & GDs

Part B: COLLOCATIONS
📌 Definition: A collocation is a pair or group of words that naturally and commonly occur together in English. They "sound right" to native speakers because of habit, not grammar rules.
Example: "make a decision" ✅ (not "do a decision" ❌)
💡 Memory Trick (V+N): Most collocations are Verb + Noun pairs. The key is learning WHICH verb goes with WHICH noun. Don't translate from your own language!
🔵 Types of Collocations
TypePatternExample
Verb + NounV + Nmake a plan, do homework, take a risk
Adjective + NounAdj + Nheavy rain, strong coffee, hard work
Noun + VerbN + Vbusiness runs, time flies
Adverb + AdjectiveAdv + Adjdeeply concerned, highly unlikely
Noun + NounN + Nfamily business, business partner
🔵 Key Collocations (Master List)
With MAKE: make an appointment, make money, make a plan, make a choice, make a mistake
With DO: do business, do homework, do the dishes, do your best
With TAKE: take a risk, take a break, take a seat, take a chance, take a test
With GET: get a job, get married, get ready, get lost
With HAVE: have a good time, have a look, have a party, have a problem, have a chance
With KEEP: keep a secret, keep in touch, keep calm, keep a promise
With RUN: run a business, run out of time, run a risk, run a marathon
With WIN: win a game, win a match, win a prize, win support
With BREAK: break the law, break a promise, break a record
With PAY: pay attention, pay the price, pay respect, pay a visit
With TELL: tell the truth, tell a story, tell a lie, tell the difference
With SAVE: save money, save time, save energy
With CATCH: catch a cold, catch a bus, catch someone's attention
With COME: come true, come to a conclusion
With GIVE: give advice, give a speech, give permission
⭐ Exam Pattern: "Fill in the correct verb" questions test collocations. Always think: make/do/take/have/get/keep/run — these 7 verbs form 80% of exam collocations!

Part C: FORMULAIC EXPRESSIONS
📌 Definition: Formulaic expressions are fixed, ready-made phrases that are learned as whole units, used in common situations, and sound natural and fluent. They are stored in memory as chunks, not constructed word by word.
🔵 Academic Formulaic Language (AFL)
Used in both written & spoken academic English. These are the "building blocks" of academic communication.

🔵 How Formulaic Expressions Improve Fluency
  • Reduce hesitation while speaking — no need to construct from scratch
  • Save mental processing time — retrieved as a single chunk
  • Make speech sound natural and native-like
  • Help ideas flow smoothly in writing and presentations
  • Build overall speaking and writing confidence
💡 Key Insight: Research shows that 60–70% of everyday language is formulaic! Learning these chunks is more efficient than learning individual grammar rules.
